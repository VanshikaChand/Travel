Welcome to Andaman & Nicobar Islands



Tropical Serenity and Azure Waters Await



Beaches & Sunsets

Relax at Radhanagar Beach, Kalapathar, and Laxmanpur. Powder-white sands and golden sunsets make the Andaman shoreline unforgettable.

Coral Reefs & Diving

Explore the underwater world at Havelock, Neil, and North Bay with scuba diving, snorkeling, and glass-bottom boat rides in vibrant coral gardens.

Cellular Jail

Witness the colonial-era “Kala Pani” prison and its light-and-sound show — a stirring tribute to India’s freedom fighters and the island’s turbulent history.

Tribal Reserves

The Andaman and Nicobar Islands are home to indigenous tribes like the Jarwa and Nicobarese. While protected, their presence enriches the cultural mosaic.

Limestone Caves

Explore the stunning limestone caves of Baratang Island by boat — an adventure through mangroves, mud volcanoes, and ancient stone formations.

Island Hopping

From Port Blair to Neil Island, each destination offers unique charm — colonial relics, local bazaars, pristine waters, and vibrant island life await your discovery.

Culture of Andaman & Nicobar Islands

Explore the traditions, festivals, cuisine, and craftsmanship of this tropical island paradise.

Traditional Dance & Music

Nicobari Dance

Performed during the Ossuary Feast (Pig Festival), this tribal dance showcases rhythmic steps, vibrant attire, and traditional music reflecting nature’s harmony.

Tribal Songs

Passed through generations, these songs revolve around sea, ancestry, harvest, and celebrations.

Festivals

Ossuary Festival

A sacred Nicobari tribal celebration with dance, feasts, and rituals centered on ancestral remembrance.

Independence Day

Observed with patriotic spirit, parades, and flag hoisting, especially at the historic Cellular Jail.

Island Tourism Festival

A 10-day cultural event showcasing local dance, food, crafts, and performances.

Cuisine

Island Flavors

A blend of South Indian, Bengali, and tribal cuisines, dishes include seafood curries, coconut rice, grilled fish, and local spices.

Coconuts, tropical fruits, and freshly caught fish dominate the meals, bringing the flavors of the ocean to your plate.

Handicrafts & Art

Shell Crafts

Local artisans craft decorative items from sea shells—jewelry, lamps, and souvenirs.

Wood Carving

Intricate carvings from driftwood and coconut shells tell tribal stories and marine life scenes.

Cane & Bamboo Weaving

Used for making baskets, furniture, and eco-friendly household products in traditional island style.

Travel Tips for Andaman & Nicobar Islands

Stay safe, respect local cultures, and make the most of your island adventure.

Best Time to Visit

Visit between October and May for pleasant weather and clear skies. Monsoons (June–September) may disrupt travel due to rough seas.

Getting Around

  • Ferries: Connect major islands like Port Blair, Havelock, and Neil.
  • Boats: Available for remote beaches and snorkeling spots.
  • Taxis & Scooters: Hire for local travel; autos available in Port Blair.

Local Etiquette

  • Respect tribal communities and avoid restricted areas.
  • Beachwear is fine on beaches, but modest clothing is expected elsewhere.
  • Always ask before photographing locals or sacred sites.

Food & Water

Enjoy fresh seafood and island-style curries. Stick to bottled water and avoid raw food in less hygienic areas.

Safety Tips

  • Check weather and sea conditions before boat rides.
  • Follow safety instructions during water sports.
  • Carry cash—ATMs may be limited on remote islands.
