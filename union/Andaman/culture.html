Culture of Andaman & Nicobar Islands

Explore the traditions, festivals, cuisine, and art forms of the serene island paradise.

Traditional Dance & Music

Nicobari Dance

Performed during the Ossuary Feast (Pig Festival), Nicobari dance involves rhythmic movements and music played on indigenous instruments. It reflects the tribal community’s harmony with nature.

Tribal Songs

Songs often revolve around themes of the sea, ancestors, harvest, and harmony, passed orally through generations.

Festivals

Ossuary Festival

Also known as the Pig Festival, it is a significant tribal celebration in Nicobar involving dance, feasts, and rituals.

Independence Day

Celebrated with patriotic spirit and parades at the historic Cellular Jail in Port Blair.

Island Tourism Festival

Held annually, this 10-day event features music, dance, cuisine, handicrafts, and exhibitions that highlight island life.

Cuisine

Island Flavors

The cuisine is a fusion of South Indian, Bengali, and tribal influences. Popular dishes include seafood curries, coconut rice, and grilled fish.

Fresh fruits, coconuts, and spices form the base of many local delicacies, reflecting the tropical bounty of the islands.

Handicrafts & Art

Shell Crafts

Decorative items made from sea shells like lamps, jewelry, and showpieces are popular souvenirs from the islands.

Wood Carving

Local artisans craft intricate items from coconut shells and driftwood, reflecting tribal stories and marine life.

Cane & Bamboo Weaving

Used to make baskets, furniture, and household items — a practice deeply rooted in island life and sustainability.
