Travel Tips for Andaman & Nicobar Islands

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Andaman & Nicobar Islands is between October and May. During this time, the weather is pleasant, ideal for water sports and beach activities. Avoid the monsoon season (June to September) due to heavy rainfall and restricted ferry services.

Getting Around

  • By Ferry: Ferries connect the major islands like Havelock, Neil, and Port Blair.
  • By Road: Taxis, auto-rickshaws, and rented scooters are available in major towns.
  • Inter-Island Flights: Limited air connectivity is available between some islands like Port Blair and Diglipur.
  • By Boat: For remote islands and beaches, local boats are the only mode of transport.

Language & Communication

Hindi and English are widely spoken across the islands. Bengali, Tamil, and Telugu are also spoken in some areas. English is commonly used in hotels, restaurants, and tourist areas.

Food & Water Safety

Seafood is a major highlight—don't miss trying fresh fish, crab, and prawns. Opt for clean and reputed eateries. Drink bottled water and avoid raw or undercooked food, especially in remote locations.

Cultural Etiquette

  • Respect the traditions of tribal communities and do not attempt to visit restricted tribal areas.
  • Dress modestly at public places and beaches—avoid beachwear outside of beach zones.
  • Do not litter the beaches or natural surroundings. Plastic use is restricted in many zones.
  • Photography is restricted at certain locations; always look for signs or ask locals.

Safety Tips

  • Follow instructions and safety measures during water sports or boat rides.
  • Stay informed about weather updates before inter-island travel.
  • Do not venture into deep waters without proper gear or supervision.
  • Always carry some cash, as card facilities may not be available in remote areas.
