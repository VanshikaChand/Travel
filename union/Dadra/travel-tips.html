Travel Tips for Dadra & Nagar Haveli and Daman & Diu

Best Time to Visit

The best time to explore this Union Territory is between October and March. The weather is pleasant and perfect for beach outings and heritage walks. Summers can be humid, and monsoons, though lush, might limit outdoor activities.

Getting Around

  • By Road: The road network is well-maintained; taxis, rickshaws, and local buses are available.
  • Self-drive: Renting scooters or bikes is a fun and scenic way to explore the coastal routes.
  • Nearest Airports: Diu has an airport; nearest to Dadra & Nagar Haveli is Surat and Mumbai.
  • By Train: Vapi railway station is well-connected and the nearest major station for Daman.

Language & Communication

Gujarati, Hindi, Marathi, and English are commonly spoken. Locals are hospitable and generally understand basic Hindi or English for tourist communication.

Food & Water Safety

Seafood and Gujarati delicacies are must-tries. Daman also offers Portuguese-inspired dishes. Drink bottled water and dine at clean restaurants or trusted food joints, especially if you have a sensitive stomach.

Cultural Etiquette

  • Respect the quiet and relaxed atmosphere, especially in churches, temples, and tribal areas.
  • Dress decently while visiting religious and public spaces.
  • Don’t litter beaches or forested areas. Maintain ecological cleanliness.
  • Seek permission before taking photographs in tribal or culturally sensitive regions.

Safety Tips

  • The region is generally safe, but remain cautious with valuables in crowded beach areas.
  • Avoid isolated beaches at night and follow local guidance.
  • Use helmets if riding rented bikes or scooters.
  • Carry cash, as digital payments may not be accepted in remote villages or tribal areas.

Emergency Contacts

  • Police: 100
  • Ambulance: 108
  • Fire Services: 101
  • Tourism Helpline: 1800-11-1363
