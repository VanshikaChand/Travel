Welcome to Dadra & Nagar Haveli and Daman & Diu



Where Forests, Forts & Seaside Serenity Meet



Tribal Forest Escapes

Explore the lush jungles of Silvassa, home to tribal communities and exotic wildlife. Trek through nature parks and enjoy the peace of forested retreats.

Portuguese Forts

Visit majestic structures like Diu Fort and Moti Daman Fort, echoing centuries of colonial legacy. Enjoy sea views from ramparts lined with cannons and history.

Uncrowded Beaches

Relax at Jampore, Devka, and Nagoa Beaches. Clean, peaceful, and lined with palm trees — these coasts are perfect for sunset walks and beach picnics.

Cultural Blend

The region blends tribal traditions with Portuguese influence. From festivals to architecture and cuisine — you’ll experience a mix of heritage and hospitality.

Wildlife & Gardens

Spend time at Vasona Lion Safari Park or the Butterfly Park. Gardens like Hirwa Van and Deer Park offer family-friendly escapes into nature.

Seafood & Street Life

Savor coastal flavors — spicy prawn curries, fish fry, and Portuguese desserts. Walk through markets in Diu and Silvassa for a lively, local experience.

Heritage, Beaches & Tribal Charm

Daman

Devka Beach

Popular beach with rocky shores, food stalls, and musical fountains nearby.

Best Time: Oct – Mar

Note: Avoid swimming due to rocky surface

Fort of Moti Daman

Massive Portuguese-era fort with a lighthouse and colonial churches.

Best Time: Nov – Feb

Note: Great for photography lovers

Jampore Beach

Clean and peaceful beach ideal for horse rides and parasailing.

Best Time: Oct – Apr

Note: Sunset views are stunning

Diu

Diu Fort

Sea-facing Portuguese fort with cannons, lighthouse, and sea views.

Best Time: Oct – Mar

Note: Free entry

Naida Caves

A network of mysterious caves with natural sunlight patterns—perfect for exploration.

Best Time: Morning hours

Note: Wear comfortable shoes

Nagoa Beach

Crescent-shaped beach known for water sports and palm-lined shore.

Best Time: Nov – Apr

Note: Safe for swimming

Culture of Dadra & Nagar Haveli and Daman & Diu

A confluence of tribal vibrancy and colonial grace, shaped by nature and history.

Traditional Dance & Music

Taren & Varlid

Folk dances like Taren and Varlid are performed by tribal communities in Dadra & Nagar Haveli, celebrating harvests and nature.

Portuguese-Inspired Music

Daman & Diu reflects a rich Portuguese influence through instruments like guitar, mandolin, and rhythmic dances such as Mando and Dulpod.

Festivals

Nariyal Poornima

A coastal ritual thanking the sea god, marked with processions and coconut offerings.

Diwaso

A vibrant tribal festival in Dadra & Nagar Haveli involving traditional games, dances, and feasting.

Feasts of St. Francis & Our Lady

Catholic processions, fireworks, and fairs showcase the colonial culture in Diu and Daman.

Cuisine

Tribal & Coastal Fusion

From spicy tribal fare to seafood-based Portuguese dishes, the cuisine reflects rich diversity. Local favorites include fish curry, pork vindaloo, jaggery sweets, and coconut drinks.

Toddy (palm wine), roasted tapioca, and banana leaf meals are staples among the tribal populations.

Handicrafts & Art

Bamboo & Cane Crafts

Local artisans create baskets, trays, and mats using bamboo and cane — both functional and decorative.

Shell Crafts

From lamps to jewelry, seashells are artistically shaped into beautiful souvenirs across the coastal belt.

Embroidered Textiles

Portuguese-style embroidered cloth and lacework are still practiced in homes, especially in Daman & Diu.
