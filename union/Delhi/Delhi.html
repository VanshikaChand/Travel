Welcome to Delhi



Heart of India - Where History Meets Modernity



Historical Monuments

Explore landmarks like Red Fort, Qutub Minar, Humayun’s Tomb, and India Gate — each echoing Delhi’s rich Mughal and colonial past with grandeur and grace.

Street Food Paradise

From spicy chaat in Chandni Chowk to butter-loaded parathas in Paranthe Wali Gali, Delhi is a foodie’s dream where every bite tells a story of taste and tradition.

Cultural Melting Pot

With diverse communities from all over India, Delhi celebrates every festival with zeal — from Diwali and Eid to Christmas and Gurpurab — a true reflection of unity.

Shopping & Bazaars

Get lost in the vibrant bazaars of Sarojini Nagar, Janpath, and Dilli Haat. Discover handlooms, accessories, street fashion, and artifacts at affordable prices.

Modern Marvels

Witness the modern side of Delhi with Lotus Temple, Akshardham, and sprawling malls of Saket and Vasant Kunj offering entertainment, architecture, and luxury.

Vibrant Nightlife

From cafes in Hauz Khas to rooftop lounges in Connaught Place, Delhi’s nightlife is buzzing with music, dance, and flavors from all over the world.

Culture of Delhi

Experience Delhi’s vibrant culture through its monuments, festivals, cuisine, and artistic legacy.

Traditional Dance & Music

Fusion & Classical Forms

Delhi hosts a variety of classical dance performances including Kathak and Bharatanatyam, often showcased at cultural centers like Kamani Auditorium and India Habitat Centre.

Music Scene

From Sufi nights at Nizamuddin Dargah to contemporary indie bands in Hauz Khas, Delhi offers a diverse and rich musical landscape.

Festivals

Diwali

The festival of lights is celebrated with grandeur, vibrant decorations, and dazzling firework displays across the city.

Republic Day

Held on Rajpath, this grand parade showcases India's cultural and military heritage with floats and performances.

Eid-ul-Fitr

Celebrated with joy and feasting, particularly around Jama Masjid, with markets and mosques beautifully lit up.

Cuisine

Flavors of Delhi

Delhi’s cuisine is a delightful mix of Mughlai, Punjabi, and street food. Popular dishes include butter chicken, chole bhature, kebabs, and chaats from Chandni Chowk.

Food is a cultural experience here — from fine dining at Connaught Place to street stalls near India Gate.

Handicrafts & Art

Meenakari Art

Exquisite enamel work seen in jewelry and artifacts sold in Delhi’s traditional bazaars.

Paper Mache Crafts

Beautiful decorative items crafted using traditional techniques, often found at Dilli Haat and craft melas.

Handloom & Textiles

From Khadi to Jamdani, Delhi markets showcase elegant fabrics and traditional weaves.

Travel Tips for Delhi

Make the most of your visit to India's capital with these essential travel tips.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Delhi is from October to March when the weather is cool and ideal for sightseeing. Avoid the scorching summer months of April to June, and be cautious during peak winter smog in December.

Getting Around

  • By Metro: The Delhi Metro is fast, clean, and the most convenient way to travel around the city.
  • By Auto: Auto-rickshaws are available across Delhi, but ensure the meter is used or negotiate the fare.
  • App-based Cabs: Ola and Uber are widely available and safer for longer or late-night travel.
  • By Bus: DTC buses connect most areas, but they can be crowded during peak hours.

Language & Communication

Hindi is the primary language, but English is widely understood, especially in tourist and business areas. Learning a few Hindi phrases like “Namaste” (Hello) or “Shukriya” (Thank you) can be helpful.

Food & Water Safety

Delhi is known for its rich street food culture. Try dishes like Chole Bhature, Parathas, and Kebabs from reputed stalls. Always drink bottled water and eat freshly cooked food. Avoid cut fruits or raw items from street vendors.

Cultural Etiquette

  • Respect local customs, especially at religious sites like temples and mosques.
  • Dress modestly when visiting cultural or religious places.
  • Remove footwear before entering someone’s home or a place of worship.
  • Always ask permission before photographing people, especially in sensitive areas.

Safety Tips

  • Avoid isolated areas after dark and stay in well-populated places.
  • Use official or app-based transport options.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded places like markets and metro stations.
  • Carry a copy of your ID and important numbers with you at all times.

Emergency Contacts

  • Police: 100
  • Ambulance: 102
  • Fire: 101
  • Delhi Tourism Helpline: 1363 / 1800-11-1363
