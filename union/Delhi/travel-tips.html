Travel Tips for Delhi

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Delhi is from October to March when the weather is cool and ideal for sightseeing. Avoid the scorching summer months of April to June, and be cautious during peak winter smog in December.

Getting Around

  • By Metro: The Delhi Metro is fast, clean, and the most convenient way to travel around the city.
  • By Auto: Auto-rickshaws are available across Delhi, but ensure the meter is used or negotiate the fare.
  • App-based Cabs: Ola and Uber are widely available and safer for longer or late-night travel.
  • By Bus: DTC buses connect most areas, but they can be crowded during peak hours.

Language & Communication

Hindi is the primary language, but English is widely understood, especially in tourist and business areas. Learning a few Hindi phrases like “Namaste” (Hello) or “Shukriya” (Thank you) can be helpful.

Food & Water Safety

Delhi is known for its rich street food culture. Try dishes like Chole Bhature, Parathas, and Kebabs from reputed stalls. Always drink bottled water and eat freshly cooked food. Avoid cut fruits or raw items from street vendors.

Cultural Etiquette

  • Respect local customs, especially at religious sites like temples and mosques.
  • Dress modestly when visiting cultural or religious places.
  • Remove footwear before entering someone’s home or a place of worship.
  • Always ask permission before photographing people, especially in sensitive areas.

Safety Tips

  • Avoid isolated areas after dark and stay in well-populated places.
  • Use official or app-based transport options.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded places like markets and metro stations.
  • Carry a copy of your ID and important numbers with you at all times.

Emergency Contacts

  • Police: 100
  • Ambulance: 102
  • Fire: 101
  • Delhi Tourism Helpline: 1363 / 1800-11-1363
