Culture of Delhi

Experience Delhi’s vibrant culture through its monuments, festivals, cuisine, and artistic legacy.

Traditional Dance & Music

Fusion & Classical Forms

Delhi hosts a variety of classical dance performances including Kathak and Bharatanatyam, often showcased at cultural centers like Kamani Auditorium and India Habitat Centre.

Music Scene

From Sufi nights at Nizamuddin Dargah to contemporary indie bands in Hauz Khas, Delhi offers a diverse and rich musical landscape.

Festivals

Diwali

The festival of lights is celebrated with grandeur, vibrant decorations, and dazzling firework displays across the city.

Republic Day

Held on Rajpath, this grand parade showcases India's cultural and military heritage with floats and performances.

Eid-ul-Fitr

Celebrated with joy and feasting, particularly around Jama Masjid, with markets and mosques beautifully lit up.

Cuisine

Flavors of Delhi

Delhi’s cuisine is a delightful mix of Mughlai, Punjabi, and street food. Popular dishes include butter chicken, chole bhature, kebabs, and chaats from Chandni Chowk.

Food is a cultural experience here — from fine dining at Connaught Place to street stalls near India Gate.

Handicrafts & Art

Meenakari Art

Exquisite enamel work seen in jewelry and artifacts sold in Delhi’s traditional bazaars.

Paper Mache Crafts

Beautiful decorative items crafted using traditional techniques, often found at Dilli Haat and craft melas.

Handloom & Textiles

From Khadi to Jamdani, Delhi markets showcase elegant fabrics and traditional weaves.
