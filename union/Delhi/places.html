Travel Delhi

The Heart of India



Central Delhi

India Gate

A war memorial and a popular picnic spot lit beautifully at night.

Best Time: Oct – Mar

Note: Open 24x7

Rashtrapati Bhavan

Official residence of the President, known for Mughal Gardens and architecture.

Best Time: Feb (Mughal Gardens open)

Note: Entry via booking

Connaught Place

Colonial-style shopping and dining hub in the heart of Delhi.

Best Time: Year-round

Note: Open all days except Sundays (some stores)



South Delhi

Qutub Minar

UNESCO World Heritage Site, tallest brick minaret in the world.

Best Time: Oct – Mar

Note: Open daily

Lotus Temple

Architectural marvel and Baháʼí House of Worship known for peace and silence.

Best Time: Oct – Mar

Closed: Mondays

Hauz Khas Village

Trendy blend of ancient ruins, cafes, and boutiques around a lake.

Best Time: Evenings & weekends

Note: Lively nightlife



Old Delhi

Red Fort

Iconic Mughal fort where Independence Day speeches are held.

Best Time: Oct – Mar

Closed: Mondays

Jama Masjid

One of the largest mosques in India with stunning Mughal architecture.

Best Time: Early morning or evening

Note: Dress modestly

Chandni Chowk

Bustling market for food, spices, jewelry, and traditional Indian wear.

Best Time: Weekdays (less crowded)

Note: Narrow lanes, best explored on foot or rickshaw
