Culture of Jammu & Kashmir

Explore the timeless beauty of Kashmiri arts, spiritual harmony, traditional music, and rich culinary legacy.

Traditional Dance & Music

Rouf & Hafiza

Rouf is a graceful dance performed by Kashmiri women during festivals. Hafiza is performed during weddings with rhythmic claps and footwork.

Sufiana Music

Rooted in Sufi tradition, this musical genre uses instruments like santoor, rabab, and sitar with spiritual lyrics and classical ragas.

Festivals

Tulip Festival

Held in April at the Indira Gandhi Tulip Garden, this festival welcomes spring with vibrant blooms and cultural events.

Eid

Both Eid-ul-Fitr and Eid-ul-Adha are celebrated with feasts, new clothes, and community prayers at grand mosques like Hazratbal.

Baisakhi

Celebrated widely in Jammu, it marks the harvest and is accompanied by folk music, fairs, and river rituals.

Cuisine

Wazwan & Kahwa

The crown jewel of Kashmiri cuisine is the multi-course **Wazwan** featuring dishes like Rogan Josh, Yakhni, Gushtaba, and Tabak Maaz. Served at weddings and royal gatherings.

Kahwa (spiced green tea with almonds and saffron) is a signature beverage, often enjoyed in copper samovars.

Handicrafts & Art

Pashmina Shawls

Made from the finest wool of Changthangi goats, these luxurious shawls are soft, warm, and handwoven with intricate patterns.

Papier Mâché

Decorative art made from paper pulp, hand-painted with floral designs. Boxes, vases, and lamps are common creations.

Silk Carpets

Exquisite hand-knotted carpets with Persian-style motifs, crafted in Srinagar and surrounding villages.
