Welcome to Jammu & Kashmir



Paradise on Earth



Heavenly Landscapes

From the lush meadows of Gulmarg and Pahalgam to the snowy peaks of Sonamarg, every view in Jammu & Kashmir is a living postcard.

Dal Lake & Shikara Rides

Float through calm waters in Srinagar on colorful shikaras. Stay in traditional houseboats and enjoy stunning Himalayan reflections.

Mughal Gardens

Walk through Nishat Bagh, Shalimar Bagh, and Chashme Shahi — terraced gardens that bloom with tulips, fountains, and ancient architecture.

Adventure Sports

Try skiing in Gulmarg, white water rafting in Lidder, trekking in the Himalayas, or paragliding over valleys for adrenaline-filled experiences.

Spiritual Sites

Visit Vaishno Devi Temple in Jammu and the serene Hazratbal Shrine or Shankaracharya Temple in Srinagar — spiritual journeys in majestic settings.

Kashmiri Culture

Admire handcrafted Pashmina shawls, intricate papier-mâché art, and savor Wazwan cuisine — a royal feast rich in spices and tradition.

Culture of Jammu & Kashmir

Explore the timeless beauty of Kashmiri arts, spiritual harmony, traditional music, and rich culinary legacy.

Traditional Dance & Music

Rouf & Hafiza

Rouf is a graceful dance performed by Kashmiri women during festivals. Hafiza is performed during weddings with rhythmic claps and footwork.

Sufiana Music

Rooted in Sufi tradition, this musical genre uses instruments like santoor, rabab, and sitar with spiritual lyrics and classical ragas.

Festivals

Tulip Festival

Held in April at the Indira Gandhi Tulip Garden, this festival welcomes spring with vibrant blooms and cultural events.

Eid

Both Eid-ul-Fitr and Eid-ul-Adha are celebrated with feasts, new clothes, and community prayers at grand mosques like Hazratbal.

Baisakhi

Celebrated widely in Jammu, it marks the harvest and is accompanied by folk music, fairs, and river rituals.

Cuisine

Wazwan & Kahwa

The crown jewel of Kashmiri cuisine is the multi-course Wazwan featuring dishes like Rogan Josh, Yakhni, Gushtaba, and Tabak Maaz. Served at weddings and royal gatherings.

Kahwa (spiced green tea with almonds and saffron) is a signature beverage, often enjoyed in copper samovars.

Handicrafts & Art

Pashmina Shawls

Made from the finest wool of Changthangi goats, these luxurious shawls are soft, warm, and handwoven with intricate patterns.

Papier Mâché

Decorative art made from paper pulp, hand-painted with floral designs. Boxes, vases, and lamps are common creations.

Silk Carpets

Exquisite hand-knotted carpets with Persian-style motifs, crafted in Srinagar and surrounding villages.

Travel Tips for Jammu & Kashmir

Essential advice to explore the beauty and culture of J&K safely and smoothly.

Best Time to Visit

For Kashmir Valley, visit from March to October to enjoy tulips, lush gardens, and pleasant weather. For snowfall and skiing, December to February is ideal. Jammu is best explored between September and April.

Getting Around

  • By Air: Srinagar and Jammu airports connect to major Indian cities.
  • By Road: Taxis and buses operate between tourist destinations efficiently.
  • Local Travel: Use cabs, auto-rickshaws, and shared jeeps within cities.
  • Houseboats & Shikaras: Enjoy Shikara rides or overnight stays on Dal Lake houseboats in Srinagar.

Language & Communication

Kashmiri and Dogri are regional languages. Hindi and Urdu are widely spoken. English is commonly used in hotels and tourist areas. Greeting with “Asalam-u-Alaikum” or “Namaste” is appreciated.

Food & Water Safety

Savor Rogan Josh, Wazwan, Dum Aloo, Kahwa, and more. In Jammu, try Rajma Chawal and Kaladi Kulcha. Drink bottled water and avoid raw food in remote areas.

Cultural Etiquette

  • Dress modestly, especially in religious and rural regions.
  • Remove shoes before entering mosques, temples, and shrines.
  • Always ask before photographing people, especially women or at religious sites.
  • Use your right hand for handshakes or offering items.

Safety Tips

  • Follow official travel advisories and local news.
  • Avoid border areas or restricted zones without permission.
  • Use local guides for trekking or offbeat travel.
  • Carry warm clothing, even in spring, for high-altitude areas.
