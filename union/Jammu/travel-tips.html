Travel Tips for Jammu & Kashmir

Best Time to Visit

For Kashmir Valley, the best time is from March to October — enjoy tulip gardens, green meadows, and pleasant weather. For snowfall and skiing, visit from December to February. Jammu is ideal between September and April for temple visits and pilgrimages.

Getting Around

  • By Air: Srinagar and Jammu have domestic airports with regular flights from major cities.
  • By Road: Well-connected buses and taxis operate between cities and tourist spots.
  • Local Travel: In cities like Srinagar, use auto-rickshaws, cabs, or shared jeeps.
  • Houseboats & Shikaras: In Srinagar, take Shikara rides or stay in Dal Lake houseboats for a unique experience.

Language & Communication

Kashmiri and Dogri are regional languages. Urdu and Hindi are widely understood, and English is spoken in hotels and tourist hubs. A smile and “Asalam-u-Alaikum” or “Namaste” go a long way in making connections.

Food & Water Safety

Relish Rogan Josh, Dum Aloo, Wazwan, Kahwa, and Kashmiri bread. In Jammu, try Rajma Chawal and Kaladi Kulcha. Prefer bottled water and eat at reputable places. Avoid raw food in remote areas.

Cultural Etiquette

  • Dress modestly, especially in religious and rural areas.
  • Remove shoes before entering shrines, mosques, or temples.
  • Respect local traditions; ask before photographing people, especially women or religious sites.
  • Use your right hand for greetings or giving something to others.

Safety Tips

  • Stay updated with local news and follow travel advisories before visiting.
  • Avoid restricted or sensitive border areas unless permitted.
  • Travel with local guides in offbeat or trekking zones.
  • Carry warm clothes even in spring, especially in higher altitudes.
