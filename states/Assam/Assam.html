Welcome to Assam



Where Tea Gardens and Rhinos Reign



Lush Tea Gardens

Assam is famous worldwide for its sprawling tea estates. Visit places like Jorhat and Sivasagar to explore vast green plantations and learn about tea processing.

Brahmaputra River

Experience the grandeur of the mighty Brahmaputra River with river cruises, ferry rides, and stunning sunset views along its banks.

Wildlife & National Parks

Explore Kaziranga and Manas National Parks, home to the Indian one-horned rhinoceros, wild elephants, and rich biodiversity.

Rich Culture & Festivals

Enjoy festivals like Bihu, with traditional dance, music, and delicious Assamese cuisine that celebrates the state’s unique heritage.

Architectural Heritage

Visit historic sites like the Rang Ghar and Talatal Ghar, showcasing Assam’s rich Ahom dynasty architecture and legacy.

Handicrafts & Textiles

Discover Assam’s famous silk like Muga and Eri, along with exquisite handwoven textiles and traditional crafts sold by local artisans.

Assam Culture

Assam’s culture is a graceful blend of tribal, Ahom, and Vaishnavite traditions — expressed through rhythmic dance forms, handloom textiles, lush tea estates, and festivals rooted in nature.

Traditional Dance & Music

Bihu & Sattriya Dance

Bihu is a high-energy folk dance symbolizing springtime and joy. Sattriya, a classical dance by Vaishnav monks, narrates mythological tales with elegant expressions.

Melodic Folk Instruments

Instruments like the pepa (buffalo horn), dhol, toka, and gogona accompany traditional music, especially during festivals like Rongali Bihu.

Festivals

Rongali Bihu

Celebrated in April, Rongali Bihu marks Assamese New Year with songs, dance, feasting, and rituals to honor cattle and nature.

Ambubachi Mela

Held at Kamakhya Temple in Guwahati, this tantric fertility festival celebrates the menstruation of the goddess Shakti.

Ali-Ai-Ligang

Observed by the Mising tribe, it welcomes the sowing season with dance, rice beer, and traditional cuisine.

Cuisine

Fresh, Simple & Aromatic

Assamese food is light yet flavorful, using local herbs, mustard, bamboo shoots, and minimal spices. Rice and fish are staples.

Popular dishes include Masor Tenga (sour fish curry), Khar, Duck Curry, Aloo Pitika, and Pithas (rice cakes) during Bihu.

Handicrafts & Art

Muga & Eri Silk

Assam is famous for golden Muga silk and Eri silk, handwoven into mekhela chadors, scarves, and shawls with traditional motifs.

Jaapi & Cane Craft

Jaapi (bamboo and palm-leaf hat) and cane products like baskets, mats, and stools reflect rural ingenuity and artistry.

Gamusa & Embroidery

A red-bordered white cloth offered as a mark of respect during Bihu and rituals, handwoven with love in every Assamese home.

Travel Tips for Assam

Explore the land of rivers, rhinos, and rich culture—travel smart across Assam.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Assam is from October to April. Winter is ideal for visiting national parks like Kaziranga and enjoying the Bihu festivals. Monsoons (May–September) bring lush greenery but also heavy rains and flooding in some areas.

Getting Around

  • Buses & Shared Taxis: Available between towns and villages, especially from Guwahati.
  • Boats: Essential for reaching riverine islands like Majuli.
  • Auto Rickshaws: Common in towns; rates are often negotiable.
  • Trains & Flights: Guwahati is the main hub with strong connectivity across India.

Language & Communication

Assamese is the primary language, but Hindi and English are widely understood in urban and tourist areas. In tribal regions, locals may speak Bodo, Mishing, or Karbi. A warm “Namaskar” is a friendly way to greet people.

Food & Water Safety

Savor dishes like Khar, Masor Tenga (sour fish curry), and Pithas. Street food is flavorful—eat from clean vendors. Avoid tap water—opt for bottled or purified water, especially in remote regions.

Cultural Etiquette

  • Always remove footwear before entering temples or homes.
  • Respect tribal customs and dress modestly in villages and religious places.
  • Do not disturb wildlife or pluck tea leaves without permission on plantations.
  • Seek permission before taking photographs of locals or traditional ceremonies.

Safety Tips

  • Assam is generally safe, but avoid remote forested areas at night.
  • Travel with a guide when exploring tribal areas or wildlife zones.
  • Carry cash for rural areas—ATMs may not be accessible everywhere.
  • During festivals and temple visits, beware of pickpocketing in crowds.
