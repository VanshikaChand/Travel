Welcome to Sikkim



Journey to the Land of Clouds and Serenity



Majestic Himalayan Views

Witness the breathtaking peaks of Kanchenjunga, the world’s third highest mountain, and explore serene landscapes perfect for trekking and nature walks.

Rich Buddhist Culture

Visit ancient monasteries like Rumtek and Pemayangtse, and immerse yourself in peaceful prayer rituals, vibrant festivals, and traditional Buddhist art.

Floral and Faunal Diversity

Explore the unique biodiversity of the region, home to orchids, rhododendrons, and rare animals like the red panda in the Khangchendzonga National Park.

Adventure & Trekking

Trek through untouched valleys like Yumthang and Dzongri, enjoy river rafting on the Teesta River, and experience camping under starry Himalayan skies.

Festivals & Traditions

Celebrate vibrant festivals such as Losar, Bumchu, and Saga Dawa, showcasing Sikkim's harmonious blend of cultures and traditions.

Peaceful Retreats

Find tranquility in quiet hill stations like Pelling and Gangtok, ideal for meditation, yoga, and rejuvenation amidst nature’s embrace.

Sikkimese Culture

Discover the harmony of Buddhism, colorful dances, serene chants, and unique customs that shape Sikkim’s identity.

Traditional Dance & Music

Folk & Monastic Dances

Dances like Maruni, Tamang Selo, and Singhi Chham (snow lion dance) celebrate Sikkim's ethnic diversity and Buddhist traditions.

Chants & Instruments

Monasteries resonate with deep chants and traditional instruments like drums, horns, and bells that invoke peace and blessings.

Festivals

Losoong

Sikkimese New Year, celebrated with archery, dances, and prayers for a prosperous year ahead.

Saga Dawa

A sacred Buddhist festival marking the birth, enlightenment, and death of Lord Buddha.

Bumchu

Held at Tashiding Monastery, water from a sacred pot predicts the fortune of the coming year.

Cuisine

Flavors of the Hills

Sikkimese food blends Tibetan, Nepali, and Lepcha influences. Popular dishes include Phagshapa (pork stew), Momos, and Thenthuk (hand-pulled noodle soup).

Fermented foods like Gundruk and Kinema are staples. Traditional butter tea is enjoyed especially in winter.

Handicrafts & Art

Thangka Art

Sikkimese thangkas are intricate scrolls depicting Buddhist deities, used in spiritual practice.

Wool Carpets

Handwoven carpets with symbolic patterns are crafted by local artisans in vibrant colors.

Traditional Jewelry

Ethnic jewelry featuring coral, turquoise, and silver is worn during festivals and rituals.

Travel Tips for Sikkim

Useful insights to help you explore the serenity of Sikkim smoothly.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Sikkim is from March to May and October to mid-December. Spring brings vibrant flowers, while autumn offers clear views of the Himalayas. Monsoons are best avoided due to landslides.

Getting Around

  • By Taxi: Shared jeeps and taxis are the main mode of transport within and around towns.
  • By Permit Vehicles: Certain areas like North Sikkim and Nathula Pass require special permits and guided transport.
  • By Foot: Ideal for exploring local markets and monasteries.

Language & Communication

Nepali, Bhutia, Lepcha, Hindi, and English are commonly spoken. English is well understood in tourist hubs like Gangtok.

Food & Water Safety

Savor Sikkimese delicacies like Phagshapa, Gundruk, and Sel Roti from reputed eateries. Drink bottled or filtered water, especially in remote areas.

Cultural Etiquette

  • Remove shoes before entering monasteries and religious places.
  • Do not point your feet toward religious objects or people.
  • Photography may be restricted in monasteries—always ask permission.
  • Dress modestly, especially in rural and sacred areas.

Safety Tips

  • Carry ID and multiple passport-sized photos for permits.
  • Keep emergency cash, as card acceptance is limited outside Gangtok.
  • Pack warm clothing—even summers can be chilly in high-altitude areas.
  • Inform your hotel before heading to remote regions like Lachen or Yumthang.
