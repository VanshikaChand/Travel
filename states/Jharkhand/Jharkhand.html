Welcome to Jharkhand



Where Nature and Tribes Thrive in Harmony



Mesmerizing Waterfalls

Explore Hundru Falls, Dassam Falls, and Jonha Falls – majestic waterfalls nestled in rocky forest landscapes perfect for nature photography and serenity.

Tribal Culture & Crafts

Experience rich tribal traditions, vibrant festivals like Sarhul, and intricate handicrafts in villages around Ranchi, Chaibasa, and Dumka.

Sacred Forests & Nature Reserves

Wander through Betla National Park, Palamu Tiger Reserve, and Saranda Forest, home to elephants, leopards, and age-old sal trees.

Rock Art & History

Visit the ancient rock paintings at Isko Caves and archaeological ruins at Khukhragarh to trace the prehistoric roots of this region.

Spiritual Retreats

Discover Deoghar’s Baidyanath Temple – one of the 12 Jyotirlingas – and other holy spots offering a blend of devotion and heritage.

Adventure & Eco-Tourism

Try rock climbing at Netarhat, trekking in Dalma Hills, or enjoy eco-tourism experiences in tribal hamlets surrounded by raw wilderness.
