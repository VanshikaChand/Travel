Try rock climbing at Netarhat, trekking in Dalma Hills, or enjoy eco-tourism experiences in tribal hamlets surrounded by raw wilderness.








Explore Jharkhand

The Land of Forests and Waterfalls

Nature & Waterfalls

Hundru Falls

One of the highest waterfalls in Jharkhand, cascading from the Subarnarekha River.

Best Time: Monsoon – Winter

Note: Steps lead to the base — wear good footwear

Dassam Falls

Scenic 10-stream waterfall located on the Kanchi River, popular for day trips.

Best Time: Oct – Mar

Note: Caution: No bathing due to strong currents

Jonha Falls

Also known as Gautamdhara, this serene fall requires a short trek through stairs.

Best Time: Post-Monsoon

Note: Carry water and snacks for the descent

Ranchi & Culture

Ranchi Hill & Pahari Mandir

Sacred hilltop temple offering panoramic views of Ranchi city.

Best Time: Early morning or sunset

Note: 400+ steps — ideal for spiritual trekkers

Rock Garden

A landscaped park made from rock outcrops near Kanke Dam, great for family outings.

Best Time: Evening

Note: Kids love the bridge and view spots

Tagore Hill

Named after Rabindranath Tagore, a peaceful spot with great city views and cultural history.

Best Time: Sunrise or sunset

Note: Bring a book and enjoy the serenity

Wildlife & Industry

Betla National Park

One of India's earliest tiger reserves, home to elephants, deer, and rich flora.

Best Time: Nov – Mar

Note: Go for a guided jeep safari

Jubilee Park

A sprawling urban park in Jamshedpur, established by Tata Steel, with fountains and gardens.

Best Time: Spring (March 3 — Founder’s Day)

Note: Enjoy evening light and water shows

Bokaro Steel Plant

An industrial icon of Jharkhand symbolizing progress and infrastructure development.

Best Time: Weekdays (by permission)

Note: Educational tours available on request








Jharkhand Culture

Dive into the tribal heartland of Jharkhand—where age-old traditions, colorful dance forms, forest-based cuisine, and vibrant festivals reflect harmony with nature and community.

Traditional Dance & Music

Chhau & Tribal Dances

Jharkhand is known for its traditional dances like Chhau, Jhumar, and Paika, often performed during festivals to depict war, celebration, or daily tribal life.

Rhythmic Folk Music

Music is intertwined with tribal identity—played using instruments like mandar, nagara, and flute, passed down through oral traditions.

Festivals

Sarhul

A spring festival celebrated by Oraon, Munda, and Ho tribes, symbolizing worship of nature and sal trees through dance and ritual.

Karma

Held during the monsoon, this tribal festival honors the Karma tree and involves songs, storytelling, and community dances.

Tusu Parab

A harvest festival celebrated in southern Jharkhand with folk songs and idols, especially by women during Makar Sankranti.

Cuisine

Forest-Rooted & Rustic

Jharkhand cuisine is simple, earthy, and closely linked to nature. Tribal households use forest produce like bamboo shoots, saag, and local pulses.

Signature dishes include Dhuska (fried rice-lentil bread), Thekua (sweet snacks), Rugra (wild mushroom), and Handia (rice beer brewed naturally).

Handicrafts & Art

Sohrai & Kohbar Art

Tribal wall art done by women using natural colors and motifs of animals, harvest, and fertility—drawn during festivals on mud walls.

Lac Work

Lac bangles and jewelry are handcrafted by tribal artisans using traditional techniques and vibrant designs.

Iron & Bamboo Craft

Jharkhand tribes create durable tools, masks, and utility items from iron and bamboo—both functional and artistic.

Travel Tips for Jharkhand

Experience the unexplored—Jharkhand’s hills, forests, and tribal charm await you.

Best Time to Visit

October to March is the ideal time to visit Jharkhand. The weather is cool and suitable for exploring waterfalls, wildlife sanctuaries, and tribal villages. Avoid peak summer (April–June) due to high heat and dry winds.

Getting Around

  • By Train: Good connectivity between Ranchi, Jamshedpur, Dhanbad, and other towns.
  • Private Taxis: Recommended for reaching waterfalls, temples, and rural attractions.
  • Auto Rickshaws: Easily available for short city rides.
  • Local Buses: Operate between towns but may not be comfortable for long journeys.

Language & Communication

Hindi is widely spoken, and tribal languages like Santhali, Mundari, and Ho are used locally. English is limited but understood in hotels and urban centers. A polite “Namaste” is universally welcomed.

Food & Water Safety

Try local dishes like Dhuska, Thekua, and Handia (traditional rice drink). Eat at clean dhabas or restaurants. Avoid tap water; carry bottled or filtered water, especially when visiting rural or forest areas.

Cultural Etiquette

  • Respect local traditions, especially in tribal villages—avoid flashy clothing or loud behavior.
  • Seek permission before clicking photographs of people or rituals.
  • Remove shoes before entering temples or religious spaces.
  • Maintain silence and humility during cultural festivals or tribal performances.

Safety Tips

  • Stick to well-known tourist trails—avoid dense forests without a guide.
  • Inform someone before heading to waterfalls or remote areas—mobile signal may be weak.
  • Travel in groups when visiting less-populated regions or trekking zones.
  • Jharkhand is generally safe, but it’s best to return to urban centers before dark.
