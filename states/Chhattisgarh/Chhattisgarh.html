Welcome to Chhattisgarh



A Tapestry of Waterfalls, Forests, and Tribes



Dense Forests & Wildlife

Explore the abundant forests of Kanger Valley and Barnawapara, home to diverse flora and fauna including leopards, tigers, and many bird species.

Spectacular Waterfalls

Visit the mesmerizing Chitrakote Falls, known as the "Niagara of India," along with Tirathgarh and Amrit Dhara waterfalls for breathtaking natural beauty.

Rich Tribal Culture

Experience the vibrant traditions, folk dances like Panthi and Raut Nacha, and handicrafts of over 40 tribal communities living harmoniously in Chhattisgarh.

Ancient Temples

Discover historic temples like the Bhoramdeo Temple and Danteshwari Temple, reflecting intricate architecture and spiritual significance.

Adventure & Nature Trails

Trek through the lush landscapes of Achanakmar Wildlife Sanctuary or enjoy boating at the serene Hasdeo River.

Festivals & Fairs

Join in the celebrations of Bastar Dussehra, Goncha Festival, and other tribal fairs that highlight the cultural richness and joyous spirit of Chhattisgarh.

Known as the "Niagara of India", this broad waterfall is best seen during the monsoon.

Unique 75-day tribal festival celebrating Devi Danteshwari with vibrant rituals and processions.

Best Time: Sept – Oct

Note: Different from traditional Dussehra elsewhere

Bastar Tribal Art

Discover bell-metal (Dhokra), terracotta, and bamboo craft made by indigenous artisans.

Best Time: Year-round

Note: Buy directly from weekly haats (markets)

Traditional tribal markets where locals trade crafts, spices, and forest produce.

Chhattisgarh Culture

Known as the “Rice Bowl of India,” Chhattisgarh is rich in tribal traditions, folk music, earthy crafts, and vibrant festivals deeply rooted in nature and heritage.

Traditional Dance & Music

Raut Nacha & Panthi Dance

Raut Nacha is a ritual folk dance by the Yadav community, while Panthi, performed by Satnamis, reflects spiritual devotion and cultural pride.

Folk Music & Instruments

Traditional music is sung during farming, festivals, and rituals, using tribal instruments like mandar, dhol, harmonium, and nagara.

Festivals

Bastar Dussehra

Unlike any other, this tribal festival spans 75 days and worships Devi Danteshwari with grand processions and rituals in Jagdalpur.

Gaon Utsav

Village festivals celebrated post-harvest include local fairs, dancing, feasting, and cultural storytelling.

Teeja

A women-centric festival observed with fasting, songs, and prayers for marital happiness and family well-being.

Cuisine

Traditional & Forest-Based Foods

Chhattisgarh’s cuisine is simple and nutritious, often using rice, millets, and forest produce like mahua, chironji, and bamboo shoots.

Famous dishes include Chana Samosa, Faraa (rice dumplings), Aamat (bamboo shoot curry), Bafauri, and tribal chutneys made from red ants.

Handicrafts & Art

Bell Metal Art

Also known as Dokra, this tribal craft uses lost-wax casting to create intricate metal figurines, lamps, and jewelry.

Bamboo Craft

Bamboo is used to make baskets, mats, wall hangings, and musical instruments with tribal motifs and eco-friendly designs.

Godna Art

A tattoo-inspired tribal painting style using monochrome dots and lines to narrate stories on walls and canvases.

Travel Tips for Chhattisgarh

A hidden gem of India—discover Chhattisgarh’s rich tribal culture and natural beauty safely and smartly.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Chhattisgarh is between October and March. The weather is cooler and ideal for visiting waterfalls, caves, tribal regions, and national parks. Avoid summer (April–June) due to high temperatures and humidity.

Getting Around

  • By Road: State-run buses and private vehicles are available between towns.
  • Private Taxis: Recommended for reaching remote tourist spots, tribal villages, or nature parks.
  • Auto Rickshaws: Common for local city transport in Raipur, Jagdalpur, and Bilaspur.
  • Trains: Good connectivity to major towns—Raipur is the main railway hub.

Language & Communication

Hindi and Chhattisgarhi are widely spoken. English is limited, especially in rural and tribal areas, so it helps to know some basic Hindi phrases. Locals are friendly and helpful if approached respectfully.

Food & Water Safety

Taste local dishes like Chana Samosa, Aamat, Faraa, and rice beer (mahua). Stick to clean dhabas and hotels in cities or towns. Always drink bottled or purified water, especially when traveling in forest areas.

Cultural Etiquette

  • Respect tribal customs—ask before taking photos or participating in rituals.
  • Dress modestly while visiting villages, temples, or forested zones.
  • Do not touch sacred objects or shrines without permission.
  • Try to support local artisans by purchasing authentic handicrafts like Bell Metal, Terracotta, or Dhokra items.

Safety Tips

  • Stick to known tourist routes and government-recommended tribal circuits.
  • Avoid remote forest travel without a local guide, especially near the Bastar region.
  • Mobile networks may be weak in forests and tribal zones—inform someone before travel.
  • Wildlife parks like Kanger Valley and Barnawapara are best visited with forest department assistance.
