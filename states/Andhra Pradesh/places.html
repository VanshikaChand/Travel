Travel Andhra Pradesh

Where Heritage Meets Natural Beauty



Visakhapatnam

Ramakrishna Beach

A popular beach for evening strolls, scenic views, and local street food.

Best Time: Oct – Mar

Note: Open daily

Kailasagiri

Hilltop park with giant Shiva-Parvati statues and ropeway views of the sea.

Best Time: Nov – Feb

Note: Open daily

Submarine Museum (INS Kursura)

A real Indian Navy submarine converted into a fascinating museum.

Best Time: Oct – Feb

Closed: Mondays

Simhachalam Temple

Historic temple dedicated to Lord Narasimha with Chandanotsavam festival

Best Time: October – March

Note: Open daily

Yarada Beach

Pristine beach nestled between hills, less crowded

Best Time: October – March

Note: Open daily

Araku Valley

Coffee plantations, tribal museum, waterfalls, scenic ghat road

Best Time: October – March

Note: Monsoon travel may be challenging

Borra Caves

Ancient limestone caves with majestic stalactites and stalagmites

Best Time: November – Febuary

Note:Open daily



Tirupati (Chittoor)

Tirumala Venkateswara Temple

One of the richest temples in the world, located on a sacred hilltop.

Best Time: Sep – Mar

Closed: Open daily (booking required)

Srikalahasti Temple

Ancient temple known for its Vayu Linga (air element of Pancha Bhoota).

Best Time: Oct – Mar

Closed: Open daily (ritual-dependent)

Talakona Waterfalls

Tallest waterfall in Andhra Pradesh, located in a lush forest reserve.

Best Time: Sep – Jan

Closed: Inaccessible during heavy rains



Anantapur

Lepakshi Temple

Vijayanagara-era temple with hanging pillar, frescoes, Nandi statue

Best Time: October – Febuary

Note: Open daily (booking required)

Penukonda Fort

Scenic hill fort, former Vijayanagara capital

Best Time: Oct – Mar

Note:Limited facilities
