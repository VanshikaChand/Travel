Punjabi Culture

Feel the energy of Punjab through its bhangra beats, vibrant festivals, soulful music, and hearty cuisine rooted in the land of five rivers.

Traditional Dance & Music

Bhangra & Giddha

Bhangra and Giddha are energetic dances performed during celebrations, showcasing strength, grace, and joy with vibrant costumes and rhythmic beats.

Folk Music

Traditional music is led by instruments like the Dhol, Tumbi, and Algoza, with lyrics rooted in love, valor, and rural life.

Festivals

Lohri

A harvest festival celebrated around bonfires with folk songs, dance, and traditional foods like rewri and peanuts.

Baisakhi

The Punjabi New Year and harvest festival, also marking the birth of the Khalsa in Sikh history.

Gurpurab

A sacred Sikh celebration honoring the birth anniversaries of Gurus with prayers, langar, and processions.

Cuisine

Flavors of Punjab

Punjabi food is rich, flavorful, and made with love. Popular dishes include Sarson da Saag, Makki di Roti, Chole Bhature, and Butter Chicken.

Lassi, a creamy yogurt drink, is a staple served in tall glasses, while desserts like Pinni and Jalebi are festive favorites.

Handicrafts & Art

Phulkari Embroidery

A traditional form of floral embroidery on shawls and dupattas, known for bright patterns and mirror work.

Punjabi Jutti

Traditional footwear made of leather, often embroidered with colorful threads, worn by both men and women.

Woodcraft

Intricate woodwork seen in furniture, carved doors, and decorative items made in Patiala and Hoshiarpur.

Travel Tips for Punjab

Your guide to experiencing Punjab's hospitality, history, and heritage.

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to explore Punjab is from October to March when the weather is pleasant for sightseeing and outdoor activities. Summers can be hot, while winters are crisp and enjoyable—perfect for cultural festivals.

Getting Around

  • By Auto & Rickshaw: Available in most cities and towns for short distances.
  • By Taxi: Ola, Uber, and private taxis are common in cities like Amritsar and Ludhiana.
  • By Bus & Train: Well-connected government and private services across major districts.
  • By Foot: Ideal for exploring bustling bazaars and heritage areas like Amritsar's old city.

Language & Communication

Punjabi is the primary language, though Hindi and English are widely understood in cities. Greeting locals with a warm "Sat Sri Akal" is a great way to connect and show respect.

Food & Water Safety

Don’t miss iconic dishes like Sarson da Saag with Makki di Roti, Chole Bhature, and Amritsari Kulcha. Stick to clean eateries and drink bottled or filtered water to stay safe.

Cultural Etiquette

  • Remove your shoes and cover your head while visiting gurdwaras (Sikh temples).
  • Respect religious customs and avoid smoking or consuming alcohol near religious sites.
  • Modest clothing is appreciated in rural and sacred areas.
  • Punjabis are warm and expressive—greet with a smile and open heart.

Safety Tips

  • Punjab is generally safe, but avoid isolated areas at night, especially in unfamiliar towns.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded markets or festivals.
  • Respect local sentiments, especially near border areas like Wagah.
  • Carry sunscreen and stay hydrated, particularly during the warmer months.
