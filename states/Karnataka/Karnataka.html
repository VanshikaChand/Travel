Welcome to Karnataka



A Harmony of Heritage, Hills, and Hitech



Royal Heritage

Walk through the majestic Mysore Palace, and explore the royal legacy of Tipu Sultan and Wodeyars in Mysuru and Srirangapatna.

Ancient Temples & Architecture

Marvel at intricate carvings in Hampi, Belur, Halebidu, and Badami — UNESCO heritage sites and ancient temple towns of Karnataka.

Western Ghats & Wildlife

Venture into the lush Western Ghats with treks in Coorg, Chikmagalur and safaris in Bandipur and Nagarhole wildlife sanctuaries.

Spiritual Journeys

Visit Dharmasthala, Sringeri, and Udupi for sectionine peace and serene temples that reflect deep-rooted faith and devotion.

Modern Bengaluru

Experience the vibrant tech capital, garden parks like Lalbagh & Cubbon, and nightlife of India’s Silicon Valley – Bengaluru.

Beaches & Coffee

Unwind at Gokarna’s serene beaches or sip freshly brewed coffee in Chikmagalur’s estates — a perfect Karnataka escape.








Explore Karnataka

Where Heritage Meets Nature

Heritage & History

Hampi

UNESCO World Heritage site with ruins of the Vijayanagara Empire, giant boulders, and temples.

Best Time: Oct – Mar

Note: Explore on foot or bicycle early morning

Mysore Palace

A majestic royal residence known for Indo-Saracenic architecture and Dussehra celebrations.

Best Time: Oct (for Dussehra)

Note: Palace lighting on Sundays is breathtaking

Badami Caves

Ancient rock-cut caves with Hindu, Jain, and Buddhist sculptures carved into red sandstone cliffs.

Best Time: Nov – Feb

Note: Great for archaeology and photography enthusiasts

Western Ghats & Nature

Chikmagalur

Hill station famous for coffee plantations, waterfalls, and cool climate.

Best Time: Sept – Mar

Note: Ideal for coffee tours and nature lovers

Coorg (Kodagu)

Known as the “Scotland of India”, it offers misty hills, forest trails, and river rafting.

Best Time: Oct – May

Note: Don’t miss Abbey Falls and Talacauvery

Jog Falls

Among India’s highest waterfalls, especially stunning during monsoon season.

Best Time: July – Sept

Note: Viewpoints offer breathtaking panoramas

Bengaluru & Urban Life

Cubbon Park

A green oasis in the heart of the city with walking paths, libraries, and colonial-era buildings.

Best Time: Morning

Note: Popular among joggers and families

Lalbagh Botanical Garden

Historic garden featuring exotic flora, a glasshouse, and annual flower shows.

Best Time: Year-round

Note: Don’t miss the Republic Day flower show

Vidhana Soudha

An imposing building housing the Karnataka legislature — a symbol of Bengaluru’s civic pride.

Best Time: Evening for lit-up view

Note: Can be viewed from outside; photography allowed








Karnataka Culture

Uncover Karnataka’s cultural depth—from Carnatic music and temple architecture to Yakshagana performances, vibrant festivals, and Udupi cuisine rooted in heritage and innovation.

Traditional Dance & Music

Yakshagana & Dollu Kunitha

Yakshagana is a vibrant dance-drama blending mythological storytelling with music, masks, and elaborate makeup. Dollu Kunitha is a drum dance by men in energetic rhythmic movements.

Carnatic & Folk Music

Karnataka is a cradle of Carnatic classical music and vachana sahitya, with devotional and folk tunes sung in Kannada using veena, mridangam, and nadaswaram.

Festivals

Mysore Dasara

A royal celebration of victory and culture featuring processions, palace illuminations, traditional music, and vibrant performances.

Ugadi

Karnataka’s New Year festival celebrated with rituals, bevu-bella, and new beginnings rooted in ancient Kannada tradition.

Hampi Utsav

A vibrant cultural extravaganza in the UNESCO World Heritage site of Hampi, celebrating Vijayanagara heritage through music and dance.

Cuisine

Rich & Regional Diversity

Karnataka’s cuisine varies from coastal Udupi and Mangalorean dishes to North Karnataka’s spicy flavors and Mysore’s subtle sweets.

Popular dishes include Bisi Bele Bath, Ragi Mudde, Mysore Pak, Neer Dosa, Udupi Sambar, and Dharwad Peda—each with distinctive local touch.

Handicrafts & Art

Mysore Paintings

These classical paintings use gold leaf and natural colors to depict Hindu deities and epics with elegance and fine detailing.

Sandalwood Carving

Intricate carvings made from aromatic sandalwood are used in décor, statues, and gifts—reflecting Karnataka’s traditional excellence.

Ilkal & Kasuti Art

Ilkal sarees with contrast borders and Kasuti embroidery are traditional handloom crafts treasured for elegance and symbolism.

Travel Tips for Karnataka

From ancient ruins to silicon cities—travel smart across Karnataka with these essential insights.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Karnataka is between October and March when the climate is pleasant. This is ideal for visiting Hampi, Coorg, Gokarna, Chikmagalur, and Mysore. Monsoons (June–September) offer lush greenery but may affect hill and beach travel.

Getting Around

  • Buses & Metro: BMTC and KSRTC buses cover urban and intercity travel. Bengaluru also has a growing metro system.
  • Auto Rickshaws & Cabs: Common in cities—use apps like Ola and Uber for transparency.
  • Trains: Good for long-distance travel between major cities and tourist destinations.
  • Private Vehicles: Best for remote areas like Hampi, Kudremukh, and coffee estates in Coorg.

Language & Communication

Kannada is the official language. English and Hindi are widely spoken in Bengaluru and tourist areas. A warm “Namaskara” (Hello) is always appreciated by locals.

Food & Water Safety

Try local favorites like Bisi Bele Bath, Mysore Masala Dosa, Ragi Mudde, and filter coffee. Eat at clean, recommended places. Stick to bottled or purified water—avoid tap water and raw street salads.

Cultural Etiquette

  • Remove shoes before entering temples and traditional homes.
  • Modest attire is expected, especially at religious sites and rural areas.
  • Ask before taking photos at religious or tribal events.
  • Respect queues and public order—especially in Bengaluru’s busy spaces.

Safety Tips

  • Karnataka is generally safe—just take standard precautions in crowded areas like markets and transport hubs.
  • Use authorized guides or apps for places like Hampi and Badami.
  • Mobile signal may be weak in forests or hill stations—download offline maps beforehand.
  • Carry warm clothes for hill stations and mosquito repellent for coastal or forest zones.
