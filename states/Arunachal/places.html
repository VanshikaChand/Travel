Travel Arunachal Prades

Land of the Rising Sun and Pristine Peaks



Tawang District

Tawang Monastery

Second largest monastery in the world, a spiritual hub surrounded by snow-capped mountains.

Best Time: March – October

Sela Pass

A high-altitude mountain pass with stunning views and a frozen lake during winter.

Best Time: April – October

Tawang War Memorial

A tribute to Indian soldiers who sacrificed their lives in the 1962 war.

Note: Open all year



West Kameng District

Bomdila

Famous for its monastery, apple orchards, and panoramic views of the Himalayas.

Best Time: March – November

Dirang

Known for hot water springs, Dirang Dzong, and beautiful Sangti Valley.

Note: Ideal for nature lovers

Rupa Valley

A quiet valley with beautiful landscapes and rich tribal culture.

Best Time: April – October



Lower Subansiri District

Ziro Valley

Home of the Apatani tribe, famous for scenic pine forests and rice terraces.

Best Time: March – October

Ziro Music Festival

Annual outdoor music festival attracting artists and tourists worldwide.

When: September every year



Lohit District

Parasuram Kund

A sacred Hindu pilgrimage site on the banks of the Lohit River, famous for ritual bathing.

Best Time: January – March

Wakro

Gateway to Kamlang Wildlife Sanctuary, rich in flora and fauna.

Note: Excellent for nature and wildlife tourism



Itanagar (Capital Complex)

Ita Fort

Historic fort made of bricks; ‘Ita’ means brick, showcasing Arunachal’s heritage.

Note: Open all year

Jawaharlal Nehru State Museum

Museum featuring tribal artifacts, historical items, and natural history exhibits.

Note: Closed on Mondays
