Travel Tips for Arunachal Pradesh

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit Arunachal Pradesh is from March to October. Winters (November to February) can be very cold, especially in places like Tawang and Sela Pass.

Permits Required

Most parts of Arunachal Pradesh require an Inner Line Permit (ILP) for Indian citizens. Foreign nationals need a Protected Area Permit (PAP). Obtain these permits in advance from authorized centers or online.

Travel & Transportation

  • Road travel is the most common way to explore Arunachal Pradesh. Roads can be hilly and narrow, so travel cautiously.
  • Buses and shared taxis are available but not very frequent, especially in remote areas.
  • Plan your itinerary to accommodate longer travel times due to terrain.

Health & Safety

  • Carry basic medicines and a first aid kit, as medical facilities may be limited in remote areas.
  • Stay hydrated and eat freshly cooked food.
  • Altitude sickness can occur in high areas like Tawang; acclimatize properly.

Cultural Etiquette

  • Respect local customs, traditions, and tribal cultures.
  • Always ask before photographing people or their homes.
  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting monasteries and sacred places.

Packing Essentials

  • Warm clothes for evenings and high-altitude areas.
  • Rain gear, especially during monsoon season.
  • Good trekking shoes and sunscreen.

Connectivity & Facilities

  • Mobile network coverage may be patchy; BSNL and Airtel work better in many areas.
  • Cash is preferred; ATMs are limited in remote locations.
  • Carry power banks and chargers.
