Welcome to Arunachal Pradesh



Land of the Rising Sun and Pristine Peaks



Majestic Monasteries

Home to the iconic Tawang Monastery, Arunachal Pradesh offers a spiritual escape into Himalayan Buddhism, with peaceful prayer chants, intricate architecture, and breathtaking mountain backdrops.

Mesmerizing Landscapes

From the snowy peaks of Sela Pass to the lush valleys of Ziro and Mechuka, the state is a haven for nature lovers. Experience serene lakes, alpine forests, and panoramic Himalayan views.

Tribal Culture

Explore the vibrant tribal heritage of over 20 major indigenous communities. Witness traditional festivals, dances like Ponung and Reh, and handwoven textiles rich in symbolism and color.

Adventure & Trekking

Adventurers can trek to Gorichen Base Camp or scale the Dirang Valley’s trails. River rafting in Siang and camping in the untouched wilderness offer thrill-seekers unforgettable memories.

Exotic Wildlife

Visit Namdapha National Park to spot red pandas, snow leopards, and hornbills. Arunachal’s rich biodiversity and pristine forest reserves are ideal for eco-tourism and wildlife photography.

Cultural Festivals

Celebrate festivals like Losar, Dree, and Mopin that showcase Arunachal's unity in diversity. Enjoy folk music, traditional cuisines, and age-old customs in a joyful, community spirit.

Arunachal Culture

Arunachal Pradesh is a vibrant tapestry of tribal customs, festivals, craftsmanship, and deep-rooted spirituality — where every tradition echoes through dance, attire, and celebration of nature.

Tribal Diversity

A Mosaic of Indigenous Tribes

With 26 major tribes and over 100 sub-tribes, Arunachal is a land of remarkable diversity. Communities like the Adi, Nyishi, Apatani, and Monpa each bring distinct languages, attire, and customs.

Their lives are deeply tied to nature, rituals, and ancestral wisdom passed down through generations.

Festivals

Losar

The Tibetan New Year, celebrated by the Monpa tribe with prayers, masked dances, and rituals to drive away evil spirits.

Solung

A harvest celebration of the Adi tribe involving songs, rituals for prosperity, and dances that honor agriculture and animals.

Mopin

Held by the Galo tribe, Mopin invokes good fortune with white rice flour, wine (apong), and synchronized dances.

Traditional Attire

Colorful Tribal Wear

Arunachal tribes wear vibrant handwoven fabrics with symbolic patterns. Men often wear skullcaps with feathers or animal horns, and women don elaborate jewelry and embroidered skirts.

These outfits reflect identity and heritage, especially during rituals and cultural dances.

Handicrafts & Art

Handwoven Textiles

Tribal artisans weave shawls and garments with rich patterns that symbolize status and tribe affiliation.

Bamboo & Cane Work

Essential to daily life, beautifully crafted bamboo items such as baskets and mats reflect both function and artistry.

Traditional Jewelry

Beads, brass, and silver ornaments signify tribal culture and are worn during special occasions with pride.

Travel Tips for Arunachal Pradesh

Plan your journey through the Eastern Himalayas with essential insights for a safe and enriching experience.

Best Time to Visit

Visit between March and October to enjoy clear skies, green landscapes, and cultural festivals. Winters, especially in Tawang and high-altitude areas, can be snowy and harsh.

Permits & Entry

Indian citizens need an Inner Line Permit (ILP) to enter Arunachal Pradesh. Foreign nationals must apply for a Protected Area Permit (PAP). Both can be applied online or at state government facilitation centers.

Getting Around

  • Road Travel: The main mode of transport. Roads are scenic but winding; travel times can be long.
  • Shared Cabs & Buses: Available between towns, though frequency may be limited.
  • Private Taxis: Recommended for remote regions; local drivers are familiar with terrain and permits.

Health & Safety

  • Carry basic medication and a personal first aid kit.
  • Stick to bottled or boiled water in rural areas.
  • Acclimatize gradually in high-altitude places like Tawang and Sela Pass.

Cultural Etiquette

  • Be respectful of tribal customs and religious beliefs.
  • Seek permission before photographing people or rituals.
  • Dress modestly when visiting monasteries or tribal villages.

Packing Essentials

  • Warm clothing, especially for mornings and nights at higher altitudes.
  • Raincoat or poncho during monsoon season (June–September).
  • Comfortable trekking shoes, sunscreen, power bank, and a flashlight.

Connectivity & Money

  • Mobile coverage is patchy; BSNL and Airtel work best in remote areas.
  • ATMs are scarce outside main towns—carry sufficient cash.
  • Download offline maps and carry physical IDs and permits at all times.
