Welcome to Kerala



God's Own Country of Backwaters and Bliss



Enchanting Backwaters

Explore the serene backwaters of Alleppey and Kumarakom on traditional houseboats, gliding through a network of lagoons, lakes, and canals surrounded by lush greenery.

Lush Hill Stations

Visit the cool hill stations of Munnar and Wayanad, with sprawling tea gardens, misty mountains, and sectionerse wildlife sanctuaries for nature lovers and trekkers.

Rich Cultural Heritage

Experience Kerala’s classical art forms like Kathakali, Mohiniyattam, and Theyyam. Attend vibrant temple festivals showcasing the state’s rich traditions and folklore.

Ayurvedic Wellness

Kerala is the birthplace of Ayurveda. Rejuvenate your mind and body with authentic Ayurvedic treatments, massages, and wellness retreats amidst tranquil surroundings.

Delicious Cuisine

Indulge in Kerala’s flavorful cuisine featuring fresh seafood, coconut-based curries, and traditional Sadya meals served on banana leaves during festivals.

Wildlife & Nature

Visit Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary and Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary for elephant sightings, bird watching, and nature walks through dense forests and picturesque landscapes.

Kerala Culture

Explore the soul of Kerala—where classical arts, vibrant festivals, backwater cuisine, Ayurveda, and temple traditions weave together a harmonious lifestyle.

Traditional Dance & Music

Kathakali & Mohiniyattam

Kerala is the birthplace of classical dance forms like Kathakali—an elaborate performance using facial expressions, mudras, and vibrant costumes—and Mohiniyattam, the graceful dance of the enchantress.

Carnatic Music & Folk Traditions

Kerala’s music ranges from Carnatic ragas to Sopana sangeetham and folk instruments like chenda, idakka, and kombu used in temple rituals and festivals.

Festivals

Onam

Kerala’s grand harvest festival, marked by pookalam (floral carpets), traditional feasts, Vallamkali (boat races), and cultural performances.

Thrissur Pooram

A majestic temple festival known for decorated elephants, drum orchestras, and vibrant fireworks drawing global visitors.

Vishu

Celebrated as the Malayalam New Year with rituals like Vishukkani (auspicious arrangement), feasting, and family gatherings.

Cuisine

Coastal, Spiced & Coconut-Rich

Kerala cuisine features the perfect blend of coconut, spices, and rice. Meals are served on banana leaves and known for their aroma and health benefits.

Popular dishes include Sadya (traditional feast), Kerala Fish Curry, Appam with Stew, Puttu & Kadala, and sweet Payasam.

Handicrafts & Art

Aranmula Kannadi

A unique metal-alloy mirror handcrafted by artisans of Aranmula, famous for its reflective clarity and ceremonial use.

Banana Fiber Craft

Eco-friendly crafts like bags, mats, and décor are made from banana plant fiber, blending creativity with sustainability.

Mural Paintings

Kerala murals depict deities and epics in natural colors on temple walls, showcasing spiritual storytelling through ancient art.

Travel Tips for Kerala

God’s Own Country awaits—explore Kerala smartly with these essential tips.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Kerala is from October to March when the weather is pleasant for beaches, houseboats, hill stations, and cultural festivals. Monsoon (June–September) is ideal for ayurvedic treatments but may restrict sightseeing.

Getting Around

  • Auto Rickshaws & Taxis: Widely available in cities and towns—negotiate fare or use ride apps.
  • KSRTC Buses: Affordable and reliable for intercity and rural travel.
  • Trains: Good rail connectivity along the coast and into the hills.
  • Houseboats & Ferries: Unique modes of travel in backwater regions like Alleppey and Kumarakom.

Language & Communication

Malayalam is the official language. English and Hindi are widely understood in tourist areas. Locals are friendly—“Namaskaram” is a respectful greeting that’s always welcomed.

Food & Water Safety

Relish Kerala’s famous dishes like Appam with Stew, Puttu-Kadala, and seafood curries. Stick to clean restaurants and homestays. Drink only bottled or filtered water and avoid ice in remote areas.

Cultural Etiquette

  • Remove footwear before entering temples and traditional homes.
  • Dress modestly, especially at religious places and rural villages.
  • Public displays of affection are considered inappropriate.
  • Photography in temples or traditional performances should be done with permission.

Safety Tips

  • Kerala is one of the safest states in India—just take basic precautions with belongings in busy areas.
  • Be cautious when swimming at beaches—follow red flag warnings and lifeguard instructions.
  • Use mosquito repellent, especially near water bodies and during monsoon season.
  • For hill stations like Munnar or Thekkady, carry light woollens and motion sickness remedies for winding roads.
